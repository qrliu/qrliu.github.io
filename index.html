about me

Hi! My name is Qinrui Liu and written as 刘沁枘 in Chinese. Qinrui can approximately be pronounced as Chin-ray following the English rule or /tɕʰìn’ʐʷə̀i/ in IPA.

I am currently a PhD student in physics at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I work at WIPAC under the supervision of Prof. Francis Halzen. My work focuses on astroparticle physics, the intersection of particle physics, astrophysics and cosmology. I am a member of the IceCube collaboration, working on phenomenology and data analysis.

I come from Meishan, a small city near Chengdu in Sichuan Province in Southwest China and did my undergraduate study at beautiful Wuhan University.
